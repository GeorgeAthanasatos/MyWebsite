Mackay Park — The Future of Quartier Concordia

A team proposal to pedestrianize the Bishop–Mackay–Guy corridor at Concordia's SGW campus. The project reclaims roadway space, expands green infrastructure, and builds a connected public-realm network to address urban heat, vehicle dominance, and fragmented public space.

Key Moves
  • Reclaim one lane of De Maisonneuve for crossings and planting
  • Transform Mackay into a pedestrian-first street park
  • Pedestrianize Bishop as a calmer connector
  • Expand Norman Bethune Square using part of the Learning Square plot
  • Integrate stormwater and canopy as climate infrastructure
  • Link all spaces into one connected public landscape system
Implementation Phases
Phase 1 · 1–5 years
Test
Temporary closures, removable bollards, modular seating, and participatory consultation to test pedestrianization safely.
Phase 2 · 10–25 years
Transition
Permanent pedestrian-priority redesign, fixed seating, curb changes, and planted zones replacing tactical materials.
Phase 3 · 25–50 years
Transform
Full street-park buildout, stormwater integration, final lane reallocation on De Maisonneuve, and Learning Square coordination.
